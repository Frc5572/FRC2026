Robot Overview

Arakkis

Overview

Arakkis is Team 5572's 2026 competition robot. The robot was designed around rapid cycling, scoring consistency, field awareness, and simplified maintenance between matches. Structural rigidity, subsystem accessibility, and optimized packaging were major priorities throughout development to ensure reliability under heavy competitive use.

Mechanical

Drive Train

Features

The drivetrain utilizes SDS MK4i and MK4N swerve modules powered by Kraken X60 motors to provide rapid acceleration, precise maneuverability, and reliable performance under load. The frame structure combines grid pattern aluminum tubing, MAX pattern mounting geometry, and reinforced bumper brackets to maintain rigidity while allowing modular subsystem integration and fast repair access during competitions.

Shooter

Features

The shooter assembly uses a rigid aluminum exterior combined with lightweight Lexan plates to reduce weight while maintaining structural stiffness. A high inertia flywheel system maintains wheel velocity during repeated firing cycles, minimizing speed loss from game piece compression and improving shot consistency. Adjustable hood geometry allows accurate targeting across multiple scoring locations while maintaining constant compression throughout the firing path.

Turret

Features

The corner-mounted turret maximizes intake area and hopper capacity while enabling continuous target alignment from nearly any field position. Vision localization using cameras and AprilTags allows the robot to maintain global target tracking and improve shot consistency while reducing driver alignment demands. The turret geartrain is simplified around a through-bore encoder system for reliable positioning and reduced maintenance complexity.

Spindexer

Features

The spindexer uses a turbo-style curved geometry designed to maintain smooth game piece flow into the magazine system while minimizing jams and dead zones. Integrated brush systems continuously guide fuel through the mechanism while the staggered structural layout increases intake volume and preserves space for electronics packaging.

Magazine

Features

The magazine assembly is constructed using milled aluminum plate structures designed for rigidity, efficient packaging, and consistent fuel routing. Precision guide geometry ensures stable transfer between the intake, spindexer, and shooter systems while maintaining controlled compression and reliable feeding behavior.

Rack and Pinion Intake

Features

The rack and pinion intake system is optimized for rapid game piece acquisition while minimizing structural complexity and maintenance overhead. Large compression wheels provide strong intake force while rigid aluminum support structures maintain consistent geometry under impact loading during matches.

Programming

Auto Shoot

Features

In order to calculate shots, we give the simulator five reference shots and it creates over 1000 generated shot results using varying parameters. The generated data is then filtered to only keep the trajectories that consistently hit the center of the hub. This allows the robot to dynamically determine accurate shooting solutions across multiple field positions while compensating for variation in velocity, distance, and robot movement.

Robot Iterations

Prototype Alpha

Initial concept exploration focusing on drivetrain validation and core subsystem architecture. Established foundation for mechanical design principles.

Prototype Beta

Integrated shooter and intake systems with refined packaging. Focused on reducing weight while maintaining structural rigidity under competitive loads.

Prototype Gamma

Full system integration with vision systems and optimized electronics routing. Implemented autonomous shot calculation and field awareness capabilities.

Final Competition Build

Production-ready Arakkis with all systems validated and optimized. Incorporates feedback from testing phases and ready for competitive season deployment.
